About Me

I'm J.V., a Web Developer - a person who writes code for apps and websites. I was born in Carson, California and raised in Bacolod City, Negros Occidental Island, Philippines. I've been travelling back and forth to both countries when I was younger and permanently moved to the Bay Area, the epicenter of innovations in science and technology, a few years after grauduating highschool.

I've always had a curious mind for how electronics work. I was often scolded for unscrewiing parts of home appliances such as (old) box TV's, VHS players, my own desktop's CPU, and camera because I was curious about what was inside and what made them "tick". When I had the chance, I would always watch my grandfather or his techincian buddy whenever they had to open an electronic appliance to fix it.

During Junior and Senior year in highschool, I learned about Visual BASIC, Adobe Flash, HTML and CSS code. HTML in particular opened a vast new horizon of possibilities for me. I got instantly hooked to the idea of being able to write code and display meaningful visual results on computer screen a moment later. I still remember how everyone used to type HTML tags and attributes using all CAPS... It was quite interesting to read. There was a gap of a few years before I relearned HTML, CSS, and got introduced to JavaScript through some classes in Skyline Community College in San Bruno, CA and San Mateo College in San Mateo, CA.

One day at one of my previous jobs, I heard from a friend about coding bootcamps that did intensive training of a specific skillset over a few months. I never thought such a program had existed until that day. I did some research, filled applications, and went to Coding Dojo, in San Jose, CA for three months. It was relatively expensive, but it was one of the best investments I ever made in my life. There, I trained in three development stacks: MEAN Stack, LAMP Stack, and iOS (Swift Language). Coding Dojo built a sense of community and teamwork between each and every student across cohorts so you could always get meaningful feedback and support from various minds. I really liked that particular trait of the Dojo.

Today, I'm seeking opportunities to further sharpen my skills as a web developer as well as use them to help myself and others to build apps and websites for purposes and ideas that I share a common ground with.
